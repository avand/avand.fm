The curriculum

At the white-hot center of every world-class set is a relaxed, composed DJ. Headroom distills decades of experience into eight focused modules—one per week—covering the technical foundations, preparation techniques, and relational dynamics that turn capable DJs into confident performers.

Week 1

Sound

Understand what sound actually is and how it exists in the physical world.

Build the mental framework behind everything else in the course. Sound as a physical phenomenon—energy moving through a medium as a pressure wave—and how the body hears and feels it. Then follow sound through the full audio chain: from a vibrating object, to an electrical signal, to a loudspeaker, and ultimately to the listener's body.

  • How sound is created, transmitted, perceived, recorded, and reproduced
  • How frequency, amplitude, and wavelength shape pitch, loudness, and sensation
  • The 20 Hz–20 kHz spectrum—what's felt vs. heard, and why systems split into lows, mids, and highs
Week 2

Signal Flow

Trace an audio signal through a system and know what each piece of gear is doing to it.

How audio is represented, transmitted, processed, and reproduced across modern systems. Why signal format and signal level make the difference between a vibey dance floor and a shouting match. Once you see audio as information moving through a chain of devices, troubleshooting and unfamiliar setups stop being intimidating.

  • Analog vs. digital, balanced vs. unbalanced, mono vs. stereo
  • Line-, mic-, and speaker-level signals
  • What's really happening when a signal is gained, clipped, EQ'd, filtered, routed, and mixed
  • Identify, trace, and diagram signal flow through any DJ, studio, or live rig
Week 3

Equipment

Reason about unfamiliar gear, understand its role, and feel comfortable on it without prior experience.

The specific gear you'll meet in the booth—from approachable controllers to intimidating standalone players—and the real tradeoffs between laptop-only, controller, all-in-one, and CDJ workflows. When and why to add a mixer, how to set a proper booth reference, and how DIY rigs scale from a travel kit to a block-party system that still fits in a car.

  • Players, mixers, and configuring a proper booth
  • Stereo fields, crossovers, dynamic range, and detail across system types
  • Thinking modularly and staying adaptable on any setup
  • Building the adapter-and-cable go-bag you never gig without
Week 4

Music

Understand how music is structured, composed, and organized—and how it's stored in digital files.

The defining characteristics of a song: notes into bars, bars into phrases, phrases into structure, and how different genres are composed and blended for variety. Then the fundamentals of digital files and why quality matters on big systems, which expose details you'll never hear on headphones or a car stereo.

  • Song structure and how genres are built and mixed
  • Pitch, tempo, and Master Tempo—and whether the beat-sync controversy is warranted
  • File formats and encoders: what compression loses—transients, reverb tails, spatial detail
  • ID3 tags for portability, less data loss, and a smoother workflow
Week 5

Workflow

Build systems that create confidence, reduce cognitive load, and induce flow during a set.

Turn a messy library into a performance instrument. File-system hygiene so you always know where your music and metadata live, track prep that unlocks ease behind the decks, and smart playlists that let you improvise or play prepared. Finish with a "pre-flight checklist"—like a pilot's—so you're locked in the moment you press play.

  • File hygiene and a library that travels between ecosystems
  • Track prep—beat grids, hot cues, and tools like Platinum Notes and Mixed In Key
  • Smart playlists by tempo, key, lyrics, vibe, tag, and acoustic similarity
  • A pre-flight checklist so you're locked in from the first track
  • Optional add-on: AI workflows that auto-acquire newly discovered music
Week 6

Mixing

Create clean, intentional transitions across genres and tempos, build and hold energy, and get the most out of any system.

You already know how to mix—now go deeper. Phrase matching, EQ-based mixing, and effects, all studied through the lens of deliberately titrating energy on the dance floor. When to reach for more than two decks to preserve the sounds holding the room, and the sound-engineering basics that make every mix cleaner.

  • Phrase-, EQ-, and effects-based mixing
  • Multi-deck techniques for continuity and energy
  • Reliable transitions across broader genres and tempo ranges
  • Gain staging, EQing for a room, and using your headphones to mix better
Week 7

Relating

Develop the awareness, communication, and etiquette to navigate high-stakes, shared performance spaces with confidence and ease.

The most overlooked DJ skill is working with people. Treat equipment like an extension of someone's body, apply the principles of consent to the decks for smoother hand-offs, and build the reputation that gets you invited back. Then turn to the crowd—meeting an audience where it is and earning the trust to take it on a journey.

  • Smooth hand-offs and booth etiquette between DJs
  • Being easy to work with—and getting invited to play more
  • "Playing what I want" vs. "playing what they want," authentically
  • Reading the room and earning a crowd's trust
Week 8

Real-World Systems

Confidently operate in real-world environments.

Bring it all together and take it into the world—clubs, festivals, renegade stages, and house parties, each with its own advantages and pitfalls. What to show up with, how to work with the system that's already there, and how to stay connected to a dancefloor you can't always hear or feel.

  • Clubs and festivals—essential gear and working with house systems
  • House parties and renegades—power distribution, batteries, and generators without blow-ups
  • Staying connected to a crowd you can't necessarily hear
  • The basics of recording and live-streaming your sets

What you'll learn

This course covers the full technical stack behind DJing—from the physics of sound to the logistics of a mobile rig. Here's a sample of what we'll work through together.

Signal & Sound

How audio actually moves through cables and air. Balanced vs. unbalanced signals, gain staging, spectral analysis, normalizing track levels, and active EQ'ing.

Cables, Connectors & Adapters

RCA, TS, TRS, XLR—when to use each, how to shop for equipment, and how to avoid the adapter pitfalls that kill your signal chain.

Speakers & EQ

How speakers convert electricity to air movement. High-pass and low-pass filters, EQ strategy, and the difference between colliding frequencies and spectral harmony.

Club Systems & the DJ Booth

Anatomy of a real club setup: DJ mixers vs. house mixers, booth monitors, connecting to the house system, and navigating shared decks with another DJ.

CDJs, Controllers & All-in-Ones

The differences between hardware platforms. USB vs. laptop workflows, rekordbox, Traktor, and how to walk into any booth and feel comfortable.

Mobile Sound Systems

Building and running your own rig—from art cars to park hangs. Battery vs. generator, power requirements, and practical field lessons.

Advanced Deck Techniques

Loops, back-spins, slip mode, playing in reverse, using more than two decks, and going back-to-back with another DJ.

Software, Backups & Track Prep

Library management, hot cue strategies, audio editing, disaster recovery, virtual audio routing, and safe software update practices.

Workflow & Practice

Laptop-only DJing, ergonomic setups, using AI as a learning tool, and building efficient habits that compound over time.

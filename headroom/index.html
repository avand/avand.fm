What you'll learn

This course covers the full technical stack behind DJing—from the being comfortable and confident in DJ booths to setting up your own sound systems and everything in between.

Here’s a glimpse into the curriculum:

Signal & Sound Quality

Optimize how audio moves through cables and air. Learn about balanced vs. unbalanced signals, gain staging, equalization, normalization, and speaker placement. Get the most out of any system and avoid common pitfalls.

Cables, Connectors & Power

Learn how and when to use RCA, TS, TRS, XLR, & banana plugs. Understand how to sanely and safely provide power to all your equipment without create a rat’s nest of wires.

Spectral Balance

Take your sets to the next level by understanding how to balance the frequencies in your mix to create a rich and engaging dance floor sound. Learn the science how a mixer’s EQ-related functions work so you can get the most from them.

Club Systems & the DJ Booth

Familiarize yourself with the anatomy and etiquette of a real club setup: DJ mixers vs. house mixers, booth monitors, connecting your equipment to the house system, and navigating shared decks with another DJ.

CDJs, Controllers & All-in-Ones

Understand the differences between hardware platforms and how to pick the best platform for you: USB vs. laptop workflows, portability tradeoffs, keyboard shortcuts, 2 vs. 4 decks.

Mobile Sound Systems

Learn how to configure battery & generator-powered portable sound systems. Prepare yourself for weddings, park parties, or art cars. and running your own rig—from art cars to park hangs. Understand how to get the most sound for your money without breaking your back.

Advanced Deck Techniques

Expand beyond the basics. Learn to make loops and edit them on the fly. Learn how and when to play in reverse, use slip mode, utilize more than two decks, and how to go back-to-back with another DJ.

Software, Backups & Track Prep

Learn some advanced ways to organize your library, use hot cues, and how to edit audio tracks for improved workflows. Understand the trade-offs between file formats like FLAC, WAV, AIFF, MP3, and AAC. Also learn to use virtual audio interfaces to record your sets and how to navigate software updates and disaster recovery.

Workflow & Practice

Learn how to DJ purely from your laptop, how to improve your ergonomics, and how to use AI to make your workflows more efficient. Weekly assignments will help you build good habits and improve your skills over time.

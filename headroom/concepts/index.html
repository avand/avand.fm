DJ Glossary

"Explain it to me like I'm 5"-style definitions of terms and concepts that every DJ should know.

A
  • Amplitude The height of a sound wave — what your ears decode as loudness and your body decodes as pressure
  • Attenuation Reducing the level of an audio signal — the controlled, intentional opposite of gain
  • Attunement Reading and responding to the room — crowd, moment, other DJ — so decisions serve the dance floor
B
  • Balanced vs. Unbalanced Cable wiring that cancels noise versus cable wiring that doesn't
  • Bars The basic unit of musical time — a group of four beats that repeats as the building block of every track
  • Beat Grid The map of a track's beat structure that DJ software builds from waveform analysis
  • Beat Sync A software feature that matches BPM and aligns beats across two decks — the button that launched a thousand forum arguments
  • Booth Monitor A dedicated speaker output that lets the DJ hear the mix independently of the main PA
  • BPM Beats per minute — the measurement of a track's tempo
C
  • Clipping What happens when a signal exceeds the system's ceiling — peaks get cut off and distortion comes out
  • Common-Mode Rejection The trick balanced cables use to cancel out noise picked up along the way
  • Crossover A circuit that splits audio into frequency bands and routes each band to the right speaker driver
E
  • Entrainment The tendency of biological rhythms to synchronize with an external rhythmic pulse, like a beat
  • EQ (Equalizer) A tool that boosts or cuts specific frequency ranges — the most used and most abused knob on any DJ mixer
F
  • Frequency The number of times a sound wave cycles per second — perceived as pitch, felt as physical pressure
G
  • Gain Staging Setting signal levels at each point in the chain to stay clean and distortion-free
  • Ground Loop An electrical hum caused by two grounds disagreeing about zero volts
H
  • Harmonic Mixing Choosing tracks whose musical keys are compatible so transitions sound melodic, not jarring
  • Headroom The safety gap between a signal's current level and the maximum the system can handle before clipping
  • Hot Cues Saved markers in a track for instantly jumping to a specific point with a button press
I
  • ID3 Tags The metadata embedded inside an audio file — title, artist, BPM, key, and any custom tags you add
K
  • Key Lock See Master Tempo
L
  • Line Level The standard signal strength used by most audio gear to talk to each other
  • Limiter A processor that sets a hard ceiling on signal level — nothing louder gets through
M
  • Master Tempo Changes playback speed without changing pitch
  • Mono vs. Stereo One audio channel versus two — and knowing the difference prevents phase issues and explains half your connectors
  • Musical Key The tonal center of a track — determines whether two songs will sound harmonically compatible when played together
N
  • Noise Floor The baseline hiss and hum present in any audio system, even with no signal
P
  • Perceptual Coding Lossy compression that discards audio detail the ear is unlikely to notice
  • Phrase A musical sentence — typically 8 or 16 bars — and the natural unit for planning DJ transitions
  • Psychoacoustic Fatigue The gradual dulling of hearing after extended audio exposure — not damage, but real and manageable
  • Pulse-Code Modulation (PCM) The uncompressed standard for representing analog audio as digital data
Q
  • Quantization Noise The noise that's created when something is rounded to a limited number of steps
R
  • RCA The red-and-white unbalanced connector on turntables and home audio gear
S
  • Sample Rate How many times per second a digital system measures the amplitude of a sound wave
  • Signal Flow The path an audio signal takes from source to destination — and the map you need to troubleshoot anything
  • Stereo Field The perceived three-dimensional space that stereo audio creates between two speakers
  • Superposition When two sound waves meet, their amplitudes add together — constructively or destructively
T
  • Transient The sharp initial spike of a sound — the "thud" of a kick, the "crack" of a snare
  • TRS A three-contact plug used for balanced mono or unbalanced stereo signals
U
  • Unity Gain The setting where a stage's output level equals its input — no boost, no cut
W
  • Waveform The visual representation of a sound wave — a graph of amplitude over time
X
  • XLR The three-pin locking connector for balanced audio
